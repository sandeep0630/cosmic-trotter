The Sound That Created the Universe

Before there was light, before there was matter, before there was even time — there was **sound**.

According to the ancient seers of India, the very first vibration that emerged from the unmanifest Absolute was the sacred sound **Om** (ॐ). This is not just a religious symbol or a chant — it is the fundamental frequency of existence itself.

Modern physics now confirms what the Upanishads declared thousands of years ago: the universe began with a sound — a vibration that rippled through the void and gave birth to everything we see, hear, and experience.

Om — The First Vibration of Creation

The Complete Meaning of Om

Om is not just one sound — it is a **triune vibration** representing the three fundamental states of consciousness and the three aspects of existence.

अ (A)

The Waking State (Jagrat)

This represents the physical world, the gross body, and the waking state of consciousness. It is the beginning of all creation — the birth of form from formlessness.

उ (U)

The Dream State (Swapna)

This represents the subtle world, the dream state, and the astral plane. It is the middle state where consciousness turns inward and creates its own reality.

म (M)

The Deep Sleep State (Sushupti)

This represents the causal world, deep dreamless sleep, and the state of complete dissolution. It is the end of all manifestation — the return to the source.

The Fourth State: Turiya (Silence)

Beyond A-U-M lies the profound silence that follows Om. This is called **Turiya** — the fourth state, pure consciousness itself. It is not a state you enter; it is what you already are. The silence after Om represents the unmanifest Absolute (Brahman) from which all sound and creation emerge.

What Modern Science Says About Om

Neurological Effects

Multiple scientific studies (including research from AIIMS New Delhi and Harvard Medical School) have shown that chanting Om:

  • Significantly reduces activity in the amygdala (the brain's fear center)
  • Increases alpha brain waves associated with deep relaxation
  • Improves heart rate variability (a marker of nervous system health)
  • Enhances focus and cognitive performance

Cardiovascular Benefits

A 2018 study published in the International Journal of Yoga found that regular Om chanting for just 10 minutes daily significantly reduced blood pressure and heart rate in participants with hypertension. The vibration creates a calming effect on the entire cardiovascular system.

The Physics of Om

When chanted correctly, Om produces a frequency of approximately 432 Hz — a frequency that many researchers believe is in harmony with the natural vibrations of the universe. NASA has recorded electromagnetic vibrations from planets that closely match the frequencies produced during Om chanting.

Om in the Upanishads

Mandukya Upanishad — The Most Important Text on Om

"Om is the imperishable sound. Om is the universe. And this is the exposition of Om. The past, the present, and the future — all that is — is Om. And whatever else there is, beyond the threefold division of time — that also is Om."

— Mandukya Upanishad, Verse 1

Chandogya Upanishad

"One should meditate on the syllable Om as the Udgitha. The syllable Om is all this. One should meditate on this syllable as the Udgitha, for one who knows this, the Udgitha becomes the fulfiller of all desires."

Katha Upanishad

"The goal which all the Vedas declare, which all austerities aim at, and which humans desire when they lead a life of continence — I will tell you briefly: it is Om. This syllable is indeed Brahman. This syllable is indeed the highest. Whosoever knows this syllable obtains all that he desires."

Advanced Om Practices

1. The 21-Day Om Sadhana

Chant Om 108 times every morning for 21 consecutive days. This traditional practice purifies the nadis (energy channels) and awakens higher consciousness. Keep a journal of your dreams and insights during this period.

2. Om in the Heart Center

Place your attention in the heart center (Anahata Chakra). Chant Om silently with each heartbeat. Feel the vibration resonating in your chest. This practice develops compassion and emotional healing.

3. The Silent Om (Ajapa Japa)

After chanting Om aloud, sit in complete silence and listen to the inner sound (Nada). This is the real Om — the soundless sound that is always present. Advanced practitioners eventually hear this inner Om constantly, even during daily activities.
